# Partner Co-Marketing Planner

Plans and executes co-marketing campaigns with technology partners, integration partners, channel partners, and complementary businesses to expand reach, share costs, and generate mutual pipeline.

## Granularity Check

> **Time**: ~10 min data prep → ~10 min Claude session → ~30-60 min customizing for your prospect. If executing a full co-marketing campaign, add 4-8 weeks for partner alignment, content creation, and execution. Input is partner details, shared audience, and campaign goals. Output is Markdown co-marketing plan with campaign framework, content strategy, lead management rules, and measurement approach.

## Process

### Step 1: Partner Fit Assessment

| Criteria | Weight | How to Evaluate |
|---|---|---|
| Audience overlap | 25% | % of their customers who could use your product (and vice versa) |
| Brand alignment | 20% | Similar values, quality standards, market positioning |
| Audience size | 15% | Their reach (email list, social, website traffic) |
| Commitment level | 15% | Willingness to invest time, resources, budget |
| Integration depth | 15% | Technical integration between products |
| Competitive risk | 10% | Likelihood they'll compete with you in the future |

Fit score:
- 80-100%: Ideal partner — invest heavily
- 60-79%: Good partner — start with lightweight campaigns
- 40-59%: Selective partnership — one-off campaigns only
- Below 40%: Poor fit — pass

### Step 2: Co-Marketing Campaign Types

| Campaign Type | Effort | Cost Share | Lead Volume | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Co-branded webinar | Medium | 50/50 | High | Education, thought leadership |
| Joint blog/content | Low | Content creation | Medium | SEO, audience sharing |
| Integration launch | High | 50/50 | High | Technical partners |
| Co-branded ebook/guide | Medium | Content + promotion | Medium | Lead gen, authority |
| Joint case study | Low | Content creation | Low | Proof points, sales enablement |
| Partner newsletter swap | Low | None | Medium | Audience exposure |
| Co-sponsored event | High | 50/50 or split | High | Brand awareness, pipeline |
| Joint product bundle | High | Revenue share | High | Revenue growth |
| Referral program | Medium | Commission/referral fee | Ongoing | Sustainable pipeline |
| Social media collaboration | Low | None | Low-Medium | Awareness, engagement |

### Step 3: Lead Management Rules

| Scenario | Who Gets the Lead | Follow-Up Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Lead from partner's audience | Both (shared lead) | Partner gets first outreach, you get copy |
| Lead from your audience | Both (shared lead) | You get first outreach, partner gets copy |
| Lead from paid co-promotion | Both (shared lead) | Whoever's product is more relevant |
| Existing customer of one partner | Both, but flag as existing | Non-customer partner follows up |
| Existing customer of both | Both, but flag as mutual | Coordinate outreach (don't double-contact) |

Lead sharing rules:
- Define "qualified lead" before the campaign starts
- Agree on follow-up timing (who contacts first, when second partner follows)
- Establish lead routing in both CRMs before launching
- Respect opt-out: if a lead opts out of one partner, both must honor it
- Report lead status back to each other monthly

### Step 4: Content Co-Creation Framework

| Content Stage | Your Contribution | Partner Contribution | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Planning | Topic + outline + audience insights | Topic validation + audience insights | Joint content brief |
| Creation | 50% of content (your expertise sections) | 50% of content (their expertise sections) | Draft content |
| Review | Review partner sections for accuracy | Review your sections for accuracy | Final content |
| Design | Your brand template or co-branded | Their brand elements | Co-branded asset |
| Promotion | Promote to your audience | Promote to their audience | Cross-promotion plan |

Co-branding rules:
- Both logos appear with equal prominence
- Neutral domain or alternating hosting
- Content should be genuinely useful, not a sales pitch for either product
- Both parties approve final version before publishing
- Clear attribution for data, quotes, and research from each party

### Step 5: Promotion Split

| Channel | Your Responsibility | Partner Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| Email | Send to your list (segment: relevant audience) | Send to their list (segment: relevant audience) |
| Social | Share on your channels (3-5 posts) | Share on their channels (3-5 posts) |
| Blog | Publish on your blog with partner byline | Publish on their blog with your byline |
| Paid | 50% of paid budget | 50% of paid budget |
| Sales team | Distribute to your sales for outreach | Distribute to their sales for outreach |
| Website | Feature on relevant product pages | Feature on relevant product pages |

### Step 6: Measurement Framework

| Metric | How to Track | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Joint reach | Combined impressions and audience exposure | 2x individual reach |
| Registrations/downloads | Per partner + combined | Equal contribution (±20%) |
| Leads generated | CRM tracking per partner source | Agreed target per partner |
| Pipeline created | Opportunity attribution to campaign | X:1 pipeline-to-cost ratio |
| Revenue influenced | Closed deals within 6 months | Positive ROI for both |
| Partner satisfaction | Post-campaign debrief | Both want to repeat |

### Confidence & Sample Size

> **Confidence Note**: Co-marketing campaigns typically generate 2-3x the reach of solo campaigns at a similar cost per partner. The biggest risk is unequal effort — one partner doing most of the work while both benefit equally. Start with low-commitment campaigns (newsletter swap, guest blog) to test the partnership before investing in high-effort campaigns (events, product bundles). Lead quality from partner audiences is typically 20-30% lower than owned audiences because the fit isn't perfect — plan for this in your conversion projections.

### ⚠️ Human Checkpoint
> Review all co-branded content with legal — ensure no conflicting claims or competitive issues. Get written agreement on lead sharing, usage rights, and campaign ownership before starting. Clarify data privacy requirements: GDPR and CAN-SPAM apply to shared leads.

## Platform Implementation Steps

### Partner Identification
1. List your top integration partners and complementary products
2. Research their audience size, content, and marketing activity
3. Identify mutual customers who could provide case studies
4. Reach out with a specific co-marketing proposal (not generic "let's partner")
5. Start with one lightweight campaign to test the relationship

### Campaign Execution
1. Create shared project plan with deadlines and owners
2. Establish co-branded design templates
3. Set up lead routing in both CRMs
4. Create campaign tracking (UTM parameters, unique landing pages)
5. Hold weekly sync during active campaigns

### Ongoing Partnership
1. Conduct post-campaign debrief within 2 weeks
2. Share results transparently (leads, pipeline, revenue)
3. Plan next campaign based on what worked
4. Build quarterly co-marketing calendar with top partners
5. Expand successful partnerships to additional campaign types

## Failure Modes

| Failure | Cause | Recovery |
|---|---|---|
| Partner doesn't promote | Unclear expectations, low priority for them | Get written commitments upfront; choose partners where it's mutually beneficial |
| Lead quality is poor | Audience mismatch or weak targeting | Better audience segmentation; qualify co-marketing leads separately |
| Content feels like a vendor pitch | One partner dominates the narrative | Equal editorial control; focus on audience value, not product features |
| Can't measure ROI | No tracking or attribution setup | Implement UTM tracking, dedicated landing pages, and CRM campaign tags |

## How to Get Your Data

- **Partner identification**: Your integration ecosystem, customer surveys ("what other tools do you use?"), industry directories
- **Audience overlap**: Compare customer lists (anonymized), compare website demographics, survey mutual customers
- **Campaign performance**: CRM → filter by co-marketing campaign → leads, pipeline, revenue per partner
- **No partner data**: Describe your product and ideal customer — Claude identifies partner categories and recommends outreach approach

## Example

**Input**: "Co-marketing plan with our CRM integration partner. We're a marketing automation platform, they're a mid-market CRM. We have 5K email list, 12K LinkedIn followers. They have 15K email list, 8K LinkedIn followers. We share ~30% audience overlap. Goal: generate 200 joint leads in Q2."

**Output**: Partnership assessment: strong fit (complementary products, 30% overlap, combined 20K email reach). Campaign plan (3 campaigns in Q2): (1) Co-branded webinar: "The Marketing + Sales Alignment Playbook for Mid-Market Teams" — you present marketing automation best practices, they present CRM pipeline management, joint Q&A. Target: 400 registrations (you promote to 5K list, they promote to 15K), 140 attendees, 70 qualified leads. Timeline: launch Week 3 of Q1. (2) Joint ebook: "The Complete Guide to Revenue Operations for Growing Companies" — you write chapters 1-3 (marketing ops), they write chapters 4-6 (sales ops), co-branded design. Target: 80 leads from gated download. Promote via both email lists + LinkedIn. (3) Integration case study + campaign: interview 2-3 mutual customers using both products, create case study highlighting integration value, promote via email + social + sales teams. Target: 50 leads from case study landing page. Lead management: all leads shared equally. You follow up on leads from your audience first, they follow up on leads from their audience first. Both add to CRM with co-marketing campaign tag. Monthly lead status sync. Budget: $2,500 each ($5K total) — webinar platform, ebook design, paid LinkedIn promotion. Expected: 200 joint leads at $25 CPL (vs. $60-80 solo).
