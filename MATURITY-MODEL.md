# MarTech Skills Maturity Model

A five-level framework for progressively adopting the 156 Claude marketing skills based on organizational stage, team capacity, and MarTech sophistication. This model is a recommended progression, not a rigid requirement — adapt based on your specific needs, budget, and strategic priorities.

---

## What Is the MarTech Skills Maturity Model?

The maturity model organizes 156 marketing skills into five progressive levels, each corresponding to an organizational stage of marketing operations maturity. At each level, you build on foundational capabilities from the previous level and add new skills that enable more sophisticated marketing activities.

**The model recognizes that:**
- Early-stage organizations need quick wins and core fundamentals to prove value
- Growing organizations need to scale repeatable processes and multi-channel capabilities
- Mid-market organizations need advanced analytics and targeted campaigns
- Large enterprises need sophisticated attribution, compliance, and orchestration
- Mature practices benefit from predictive intelligence and continuous optimization

Rather than implementing all 156 skills at once (which would overwhelm), this model gives you a roadmap: start at your current stage and progress as your team, tooling, and data infrastructure mature.

**How to use this model:**
1. Identify your current organizational stage below
2. Implement all recommended skills at that level and all previous levels
3. When you've stabilized and your team shows capacity, progress to the next level
4. Use the "Signs You're Ready for Next Level" section to assess when to advance

# Level 1: Foundation (Startup / Solo Marketer)

**Organizational Stage:** Early-stage founders, solo marketers, or small teams (1-3 people). Focus is on validating product-market fit, establishing basic marketing operations, and generating the first wins to prove ROI.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Limited budget and team
- No sophisticated marketing tooling
- Manual, ad-hoc processes
- Quick wins prioritized over long-term strategy
- Data lives in multiple disconnected tools or spreadsheets

**Goals at This Level:**
- Establish a marketing foundation with core analytics and content
- Prove ROI to justify expanding budget
- Build repeatable processes for high-impact activities
- Create enough marketing stability to move to growth phase

**Estimated Timeline:** 3-6 months (Level 1 mastery)

## Signs You're Ready for Level 2

Progress to Level 2 when you observe:

# Level 2: Growth (Small Team)

**Organizational Stage:** Teams of 3-8 marketers. Company is gaining traction, product-market fit is established, and the focus shifts from proving value to scaling customer acquisition sustainably.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Growing but still limited budget (validated by Level 1 wins)
- Dedicated marketing roles emerging (content, paid, email)
- Basic MarTech stack in place (CRM, email platform, analytics)
- Processes becoming repeatable but not yet automated
- Need for multi-channel coordination growing

**Goals at This Level:**
- Scale content production and distribution
- Build repeatable email and nurture sequences
- Expand paid channel experiments
- Establish first layers of segmentation
- Create structured processes for handoffs between marketing and sales

**Estimated Timeline:** 3-6 months (Level 2 mastery)

# Level 3: Scale (Mid-Market)

**Organizational Stage:** Teams of 8-20 marketers. Company has strong product-market fit and proven go-to-market. Focus shifts to optimizing unit economics, expanding to new customer segments, and building sophistication in targeting and testing.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Established MarTech stack (CRM, MA platform, analytics platform, CDP)
- Some marketing automation already in place
- Multiple campaigns running simultaneously across channels
- More data available for analysis and segmentation
- Organizational structure includes specialized roles (content, paid, email, ops, analytics)

**Goals at This Level:**
- Optimize customer acquisition costs (CAC) across all channels
- Build advanced customer segmentation and targeting
- Implement sophisticated CRO and A/B testing programs
- Create content strategies for multiple buyer personas
- Establish compliance and governance processes
- Begin multi-touch attribution and marketing mix modeling

**Estimated Timeline:** 4-8 months (Level 3 mastery)

## How to Use the Maturity Model

### For Individual Contributors

1. **Identify your organization's current level** — Read the organizational characteristics and see which resonates
2. **Master all skills at your current level and previous levels** before moving to the next
3. **Use the "Key Outcomes" section** to understand what capabilities you should develop
4. **Track your progress** — Check off skills as you implement them; use the "Signs You're Ready" section to assess readiness to advance

### For Marketing Managers & Directors

1. **Conduct a team audit** — Which Level 1 skills are missing? Which Level 2? etc.
2. **Create a capability roadmap** — Prioritize which Level N skills to implement in each quarter
3. **Distribute skills across the team** — Some people specialize in certain skills (email specialist owns Level 2 email skills); others are generalists
4. **Track organizational maturity** — Measure how many of the Level N "Key Outcomes" your organization has achieved
5. **Use as a hiring rubric** — When hiring, look for people who already have Level N+1 skills (you need people to grow into)

### For Executives & Strategic Planning

1. **Map business goals to maturity levels** — What level do you need to achieve your financial targets?
2. **Estimate resource requirements** — Each level requires a larger, more specialized team
3. **Plan budget and timeline** — Moving from Level 1 to Level 2 is 3-6 months; Level 2 to Level 3 is 3-6 months, etc.
4. **Use as a strategic planning tool** — "We need to reach Level 3 by Q4 to support product launch" or "We can only sustain Level 2 with current team size"
5. **Track marketing's business contribution** — Maturity level correlates with marketing's impact on revenue, profitability, and strategic initiatives

## Important Notes & Caveats

**This is a progression, not a prescription.** Organizations should adapt this model to their specific needs, budget, market dynamics, and strategic priorities. A startup in a competitive market might prioritize paid ads and CRO (Level 1-2 paid skills) before content. A B2B company should prioritize ABM skills (Level 3) earlier than a B2C company.

**Skills can be implemented out of order** based on business need. If paid advertising is your primary customer acquisition channel, you can implement Level 2-3 paid skills before Level 2 content skills, even if you haven't mastered all Level 1 skills.

**Organizational maturity ≠ all team members at same level.** It's common for a Level 3 organization to have some Level 2 specialists and some Level 4 specialists. The maturity level represents the organization's overall capability, not every individual's skill level.

**Data infrastructure is a prerequisite for advancing levels.** You can't effectively implement Level 4 skills without solid GA4 implementation (Level 1), marketing dashboards (Level 2), and CRM integration (Level 3). If you're missing data foundation, invest there first.

**Team capacity is the limiting factor, not tools.** You can have the best marketing software, but if your team doesn't have time to implement skills, you'll advance slowly. Plan for capacity; don't assume tools do the work.

**The model is constantly evolving.** As MarTech changes, new techniques emerge, and best practices evolve, this model will be updated. Check back regularly.

## Sample Organizational Progression

**Fictional example: SaaS company timeline**

**Month 0: Founding (Pre-Level 1)**
- Founder wearing all hats; no structured marketing
- Random blog posts, email list building, paid ads on credit card

**Months 1-3 (Level 1 Implementation)**
- Hire first marketing hire
- Implement: keyword clustering, content briefs, GA4 setup, UTM tracking, email subject line testing
- Outcome: Structured content calendar, trackable channels, basic dashboards
- Team size: 1 marketer

**Months 4-6 (Level 2 Implementation)**
- Hire email specialist
- Implement: email sequences, content calendar, dashboard, paid ads, lead scoring
- Outcome: Automated email nurture, coordinated paid campaigns, clean CRM
- Team size: 2-3 marketers

**Months 7-12 (Level 2 Mastery, preparing for Level 3)**
- Add content lead
- Deepen: competitive positioning, sales alignment, CRO
- Team size: 3-5 marketers

**Year 2 Q1-Q2 (Level 3 Implementation)**
- Hire ops/analytics specialist
- Implement: ABM, event programs, compliance, advanced analytics
- Outcome: Account-based campaigns, sophisticated testing, governance
- Team size: 5-8 marketers

**Year 2 Q3-Q4 (Level 3 Mastery, preparing for Level 4)**
- Deepen: predictive analytics, personalization, international expansion
- Team size: 8-10 marketers

**Year 3+ (Level 4+ Implementation)**
- Investment in advanced tools and talent
- Continuous optimization and innovation
- Team size: 10-20+ marketers
